Sampling

Sound Sampling is the process of digitally recording and reusing audio snippets from existing recordings or live sources. This project aims to provide an overview of Sound Sampling, including its history, how it works, and its impact on music production.

How it works:
The project will delve into how Sound Sampling works, explaining the process of capturing and manipulating audio snippets and the different ways they can be used in music production. The advantages and disadvantages of using Sound Sampling will also be discussed, including the potential for copyright infringement and the loss of authenticity in original recordings.

Impact on music production:
The impact of Sound Sampling on music production will be explored, including its role in creating new genres like hip hop and electronic music, and how it has enabled musicians to reuse and reinterpret existing musical material. The controversy surrounding its use, including the legal and ethical issues surrounding copyright infringement, will also be addressed.

Techniques:
The project will provide an overview of different Sound Sampling techniques, such as time stretching, pitch shifting, and granular synthesis. The features, strengths, and weaknesses of each technique will be compared, providing the reader with an understanding of how to manipulate samples to create unique sounds.
